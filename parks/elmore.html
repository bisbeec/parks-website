Welcome

The Town of Lake Elmore calls itself “The Beauty Spot of Vermont.” Located in the southeastern part of Lamoille County, Elmore is mostly forested and agricultural land. Lake Elmore and Elmore Mountain, which rises almost from the lake’s shore, are prominent features of the town.

Lake Elmore is 219 acres, and lies northwest of the village and empties into the Lamoille River through Pond Brook. Elmore Mountain is in the "Hogback Range" and its timber is mostly hardwood. The mountain has an elevation of 2,608 feet.

The park had its beginning in 1936 when the town of Elmore and local citizens made a gift of 30 acres on Lake Elmore to the state of Vermont. With modest means, a picnic and beach area were created. Today, with more than 700 acres, Elmore State Park has become a popular, developed recreation facility.

The campground has 45 tent/RV sites and 15 lean-tos. Two restrooms are available in the campground with hot showers ($). A sanitary dump station is available, but no hookups. There is convenient access to several hiking trails to Elmore Mountain and its fire tower. The day use area includes a large sandy beach, an historic CCC-built beach house with a community room that can be rented for group functions, a concession stand and cafe area, restrooms, and boat rentals.

Facilities:

Elmore State Park has a beach house that is open to visitors. The beach house has a concession stand that sells light food and snacks in addition to park merchandise.

The entire beach house is available to rent between labor day and October 15 for $450. Available for up to 100 people. Amenities include electricity, tables and chairs for 50. Fee includes day use entry. Refundable cleaning/damage deposit of $200 required on day of event.

The beach house also has a nature room which is available to rent season-long. Seats up to 50 people in private room overlooking the beach and lake. Electricity, restrooms, tables and chairs for 50 provided. $200 fee includes day use entry. Refundable cleaning/damage deposit of $100 required on day of event.

Elmore also has an outdoor picnic pavilion. The pavilion seats up to 100 people and has charcoal grills, picnic tables, and restrooms nearby. There is electricity, and the pavilion is accessible.

The cost to rent is $100 Friday - Sunday and FREE Monday - Thursday. Fee does not include per person day use entry fee. Refundable cleaning/damage deposit of $100 required on day of event.
